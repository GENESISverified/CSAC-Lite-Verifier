# Escalation Protocol

Escalation gives reviewers a clear path when an outcome cannot be accepted at the first review level.

## Escalation signals

Escalation may be useful when:

- the outcome carries high consequence;
- context is incomplete;
- the evidence record is weak;
- human reviewers disagree;
- the status has been challenged;
- the decision audience requires a stronger record.

## Escalation output

An escalation should preserve:

- reason for escalation;
- reviewer concern;
- required clarification;
- next review owner;
- reporting note.

## Principle

Escalation should make responsibility clearer, not more diluted.
