# Human Review Layer

CSAC-Lite Verifier treats human review as part of decision assurance.

A clear status is useful only when the people responsible for a decision can read it, question it, and record it.

## Why human review matters

Digital systems can produce outputs quickly, but critical decisions still require responsible interpretation.

The human review layer helps teams avoid blind reliance on automated outputs.

It also gives reviewers a place to notice weak signals, unusual context, unclear records, or a mismatch between the status and the operating situation.

## Human firewall principle

A human review layer is not a decorative approval step.

It is a trained, accountable layer of vigilance around important digital outcomes.

Its role is to connect technical status with institutional judgement.

## Review responsibilities

- understand the submitted package;
- read the status;
- check whether the context is still valid;
- identify weak signals or anomalies;
- decide whether escalation is needed;
- keep a usable decision record.

## Operating conditions

Human review must remain realistic.

Organizations should define roles clearly, avoid excessive cognitive load, respect privacy, and keep escalation paths understandable.

## Governance value

Human review turns a technical output into an accountable decision object.

It also keeps the verification process connected to the people who carry responsibility for the final decision.

## Principle

A critical digital outcome should remain understandable to the people who must rely on it.
